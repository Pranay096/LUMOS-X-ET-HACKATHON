# ENGINEER'S NOTES — INFORMAL KNOWLEDGE LOG
## Not a controlled document — personal working notes shared by R. Iyer, Senior Maintenance Engineer (22 years at Ashoka Precision Manufacturing)

**Context:** These notes were collected during a knowledge-capture interview ahead of R. Iyer's planned retirement (scheduled 2027). They represent operational knowledge not currently written into any formal SOP.

---

### On PMP-204 (Hydraulic Coolant Pump)
"The seal pitting issue, you can actually hear it before you can see it. There's a faint high-pitched whine that starts maybe a week before visible leakage — most junior guys don't notice it because the machining bay is loud, but if you put your hand on the housing you can feel a slightly different vibration pattern too, a bit more 'gritty' than the normal hum. I used to catch it early this way for years before we ever wrote the humidity-interval thing into the SOP. Worth listening for during any Zone B walkthrough in the monsoon months, not just during scheduled PM."

### On CMP-301 (Air Compressor Unit 1)
"The filter clogs faster than the official 90-day spec whenever the nearby fabrication line is running heavy grinding work — metal dust gets pulled into the intake faster than normal. If grinding load is high that month, I just check the filter early, no need to wait for a formal interval change. Nobody's documented this dependency between the grinding schedule and the compressor filter life, but it's pretty consistent in my experience."

### On WLD-077 (Robotic Welding Cell)
"The robot arm calibration drifts slightly faster in the first month after any firmware update from Kuka — I always do a manual calibration check two weeks after any update, even though the SOP doesn't call for it specifically. Caught two near-miss-quality weld defects this way over the years before they became actual line rejects."

### On GEN-450 (Standby DG Set)
"Battery health on standby gensets is the thing everyone forgets because the generator itself 'runs fine' every month — but a generator can run fine on a healthy battery and a degraded one can still crank, just slower, right up until the day it doesn't crank at all. I always recommend checking battery internal resistance, not just voltage, but we've never had the test equipment budgeted for it."

### General Observation
"A lot of what keeps this plant running isn't in any document — it's just twenty-plus years of noticing patterns. When I retire, most of this either needs to get written down properly or it just walks out the door with me. I don't think anyone's actually sat down and captured this systematically yet."
