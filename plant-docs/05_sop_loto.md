# ASHOKA PRECISION MANUFACTURING PVT. LTD.
## Standard Operating Procedure: Lockout-Tagout (LOTO) for Rotating & Hydraulic Equipment

**Document No:** APM-SOP-017
**Revision:** 2 (CURRENT)
**Effective Date:** 2024-05-20
**Prepared By:** A. Krishnan, EHS Manager
**Approved By:** V. Nair, Plant Manager

> **STATUS: CURRENT.**

---

### 1. Purpose
To establish mandatory lockout-tagout procedures prior to any maintenance, repair, or inspection activity on rotating or hydraulic equipment, preventing unexpected energization or pressure release.

### 2. Scope
Applies to all maintenance activity on PMP-204, PRS-088, CMP-301, WLD-077, CNV-112, and GEN-450.

### 3. Procedure
1. Notify the Zone Supervisor before beginning isolation.
2. Identify all energy sources (electrical, hydraulic, pneumatic) for the equipment.
3. Shut down equipment using normal stopping procedure.
4. Isolate all energy sources at the disconnect point.
5. Apply lockout device and personal lock. Attach tag with name, date, and reason for lockout.
6. Verify zero energy state (test start, check pressure gauges read zero, confirm no residual motion).
7. Perform work.
8. On completion, remove tools, verify equipment is safe to restart, remove lockout device.
9. Notify Zone Supervisor before restoring energy.
10. Log the LOTO event in the Permit-to-Work register with start and end timestamps.

### 4. Permit Requirement
A Permit-to-Work is mandatory for LOTO activity exceeding 2 hours or involving more than one trade (e.g., electrical + mechanical). Permits must be co-signed by the Zone Supervisor and EHS representative.

### 5. PPE
As per APM-SOP-014, applicable to the equipment's zone.

### 6. Violations
Bypassing LOTO procedure, or restoring energy without supervisor notification, is classified as a Category 1 (severe) safety violation and is reportable under APM-REG-001.
