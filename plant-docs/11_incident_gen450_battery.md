# INCIDENT / WORK ORDER REPORT

**Report No:** APM-INC-067
**Equipment ID:** GEN-450 (Standby DG Set)
**Date of Incident:** 2025-11-20
**Reported By:** R. Iyer, Senior Maintenance Engineer
**Work Order No:** APM-WO-2025-1042
**Severity:** Moderate (critical equipment, no actual outage)

---

### Description
During the monthly mandatory test-run of GEN-450 (required for all Critical-classified standby equipment), the unit failed to reach rated voltage within the standard 10-second start window, taking 26 seconds instead. Unit eventually stabilized at rated output.

### Conditions at Time of Incident
Routine monthly test per equipment criticality classification (APM-EML-002). Ambient temperature normal. Battery voltage on starting bank found slightly below recommended minimum (11.4V vs. 12V minimum specified by OEM).

### Action Taken
Starting battery bank replaced. Subsequent test-run achieved rated voltage within 9 seconds, within specification.

### Root Cause
Degraded starting battery bank reducing crank speed and delaying voltage build-up. Battery had not been load-tested independently of the monthly generator run, masking gradual degradation.

### Recommendation
Add standalone battery load test to the monthly GEN-450 inspection checklist, separate from the generator run-test, to catch battery degradation earlier given this equipment's Critical classification and zero tolerance for failed standby activation.

### Status
Closed. Recommendation pending incorporation into next GEN-450 maintenance procedure revision.
