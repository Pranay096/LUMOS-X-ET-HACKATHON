# INCIDENT / WORK ORDER REPORT

**Report No:** APM-INC-031
**Equipment ID:** PMP-204 (Hydraulic Coolant Pump)
**Date of Incident:** 2025-07-18
**Reported By:** K. Sharma, Maintenance Technician
**Work Order No:** APM-WO-2025-0712
**Severity:** Moderate (unplanned downtime, no injury)

---

### Description
PMP-204 began exhibiting coolant leakage from the mechanical seal housing during routine operation. Pump was isolated and inspected. Seal showed visible surface pitting and partial degradation, despite being within its scheduled maintenance interval per APM-SOP-009 Rev. 1 (180-day interval). Last preventive maintenance had been performed 142 days prior — within the interval, but seal had already failed.

### Conditions at Time of Failure
Zone B ambient humidity had been elevated (above 78% RH) for the preceding 9 days due to monsoon conditions. No humidity-triggered inspection existed under the procedure in effect at the time (APM-SOP-009 Rev. 1).

### Action Taken
Seal replaced. Pump returned to service after 6 hours unplanned downtime. Coolant supply to Zone B machining lines (PRS-088, WLD-077 adjacent lines) was interrupted during repair.

### Root Cause (Preliminary)
Mechanical seal degradation accelerated by sustained high ambient humidity. Existing 180-day fixed interval did not account for environmental conditions between scheduled services.

### Recommendation
Engineering review recommended to assess whether maintenance interval or inspection criteria should be revised to account for seasonal humidity exposure.

### Status
Closed. Recommendation forwarded to Senior Maintenance Engineer for SOP review.
