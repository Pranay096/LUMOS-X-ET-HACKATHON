# ASHOKA PRECISION MANUFACTURING PVT. LTD.
## Standard Operating Procedure: Preventive Maintenance — Hydraulic Coolant Pump (PMP-204)

**Document No:** APM-SOP-009
**Revision:** 2 (CURRENT — supersedes Revision 1, dated 2019-04-10)
**Effective Date:** 2025-08-15
**Prepared By:** S. Deshmukh, Senior Maintenance Engineer
**Approved By:** V. Nair, Plant Manager
**Reason for Revision:** Maintenance interval shortened and mechanical seal inspection criteria tightened following repeated premature seal failures traced to high ambient humidity in Zone B during monsoon months (see Incident Reports APM-INC-031 and APM-INC-044).

> **STATUS: CURRENT.** This is the active procedure. Revision 1 (dated 2019-04-10) is superseded and must not be used.

---

### 1. Purpose
To define the preventive maintenance procedure for the Hydraulic Coolant Pump (PMP-204) to ensure continued reliable operation and coolant supply to Zone B machining lines, with revised intervals to address humidity-related seal degradation.

### 2. Scope
Applies to all maintenance personnel performing scheduled or unscheduled maintenance on PMP-204.

### 3. Maintenance Interval
Preventive maintenance shall be performed every **90 days** (reduced from 180 days under Revision 1), and additionally within **48 hours** of any sustained ambient humidity reading above 75% RH in Zone B, as flagged by the Zone B environmental sensor.

### 4. Procedure
1. Isolate electrical supply to PMP-204 at the local panel. Lock out and tag per APM-SOP-014.
2. Close inlet and outlet isolation valves.
3. Drain residual coolant into designated collection drum.
4. Inspect mechanical seal for visible wear, leakage, **or surface pitting** (pitting is the early-warning sign identified in APM-INC-031 and APM-INC-044 and was not checked under the prior revision).
5. Inspect bearing housing for excessive play.
6. Check coupling alignment using straight-edge method.
7. Top up bearing lubricant (Grade: Servo Mesa 32 or equivalent).
8. **Apply anti-corrosion coating to seal housing exterior** (new step — added to mitigate humidity-driven corrosion).
9. Reassemble, open isolation valves, restore power.
10. Run pump for 10 minutes and check for abnormal vibration or noise.
11. Log completion in the Maintenance Register, including ambient humidity reading at time of service.

### 5. PPE Requirements
Safety gloves, safety goggles, and face shield (added — see APM-SOP-014 for full Zone B PPE requirements).

### 6. Known Issues
Mechanical seal pitting has been observed in this equipment during monsoon months (June–September) due to elevated ambient humidity in Zone B. Two documented premature failures (APM-INC-031, APM-INC-044) were traced to this cause. Maintenance personnel should treat any visible pitting as an immediate replacement trigger, not a monitor-and-revisit item.
