# INCIDENT / WORK ORDER REPORT

**Report No:** APM-INC-044
**Equipment ID:** PMP-204 (Hydraulic Coolant Pump)
**Date of Incident:** 2025-08-09
**Reported By:** S. Deshmukh, Senior Maintenance Engineer
**Work Order No:** APM-WO-2025-0791
**Severity:** Moderate (unplanned downtime, no injury) — RECURRING PATTERN

---

### Description
PMP-204 again exhibited mechanical seal leakage, approximately 22 days after the seal replacement performed under APM-INC-031. Inspection again showed surface pitting on the replacement seal, consistent with the same failure mode observed in APM-INC-031.

### Conditions at Time of Failure
Zone B ambient humidity remained elevated through early August (monsoon season continuing). This is the **second occurrence of an identical failure pattern** on the same equipment within a 3-week window, indicating the underlying cause identified in APM-INC-031 (humidity-driven seal degradation) was not fully addressed by seal replacement alone.

### Action Taken
Seal replaced again. Pump returned to service after 5 hours unplanned downtime. This incident was escalated directly to the Plant Manager given the recurrence within such a short interval.

### Root Cause (Confirmed)
Confirmed: sustained high ambient humidity in Zone B causes accelerated pitting and degradation of the mechanical seal on PMP-204, independent of the standard 180-day maintenance interval. A fixed time-based interval is insufficient; condition-based and humidity-triggered inspection is required.

### Recommendation
1. Revise APM-SOP-009 to reduce maintenance interval and add humidity-triggered inspection.
2. Add explicit pitting-based replacement criterion (do not wait for full seal failure).
3. Apply anti-corrosion coating as a preventive measure.
4. Add face shield to PPE requirement for this task given splash risk observed during both incidents.

### Status
Closed. **This recommendation directly resulted in the issue of APM-SOP-009 Revision 2 (effective 2025-08-15).**
